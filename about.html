About Nepali Babu

We were tired of
bread that doesn't
taste like anything.

That sentence, said out loud at a kitchen table in 2020, is the entire reason this place exists.

From a market table to four walls.

Nepali Babu started as a folding table at the Saturday market, with two bakers, one borrowed oven slot, and forty loaves that sold out by nine in the morning. We hadn't planned much past that first weekend we just wanted to see if anyone else missed the kind of bread that takes two days to make instead of two hours.

People kept coming back, then started asking when we'd open somewhere they could sit down. It took the better part of a year to find a space with room for a deck oven, and longer still to get the proofing schedule right for a kitchen that wasn't a borrowed one anymore. We opened the doors in Bharatpur in 2021, with most of the same recipes we started with and a much better grasp of why they sometimes didn't work.

The coffee bar came a few months later, once we realized people wanted somewhere to sit with the bread, not just carry it home. We still bake on the same rhythm we started with early, slow, and not particularly interested in keeping up with how busy the day gets.

How we got here

A short, mostly true timeline.

2020

First test loaves, baked in a home oven that could only fit two at a time.

2020

A folding table at the Saturday market. Forty loaves, gone by nine.

2021

The Pulchowk Road space signed, mid-renovation for five straight months.

2021

Doors open. The coffee bar follows that same year, almost by accident.

2023

The corner table becomes a regular booking spot for small private gatherings.

Today

Still closed Mondays. Still baking on the same slow schedule we started with.

What we hold onto

A few things we won't change.

No commercial yeast

Every loaf rises on a starter we've kept alive since the first market table.

Small batches

We'd rather sell out by early afternoon than bake more than we can do well.

One day off

Closed Mondays, for the ovens and for us. It's stayed non-negotiable since year one.
